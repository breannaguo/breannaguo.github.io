About Me

I am originally from Portland, Oregon, and I am currently working towards a Bachelor of Arts in Economics, with minors in Mathematics and German at Middlebury College. After taking Intro to Data Science my first semester at Middlebury, I came to find searching for patterns, anomalies, etc. in data sets incredibly fun. This lead to my interest in data science, which I have been able to pursuit by doing research with Professor Rao and Professor Gauvin-Coulombe. I have also be able to share this passion with others by starting a Women in Data Science chapter at Middlebury College.

Research

Starting in Summer 2022, I began working with Professor Rao and Professor Gauvin-Coulombe as a Research Assitant. The research investigates how natural disasters impact county-level labor markets. I've worked on several projects, including scraping and wrangling satellite data to get instances of natural fires in the US from 2000-2020, wrangling migration data by US county, and running regressions using a local projection method to determine the impact of fires on county migration.

Women in Data Science

After falling in love with data science, I noticed how few of my friends were aware of the discipline. I began to work with Women in Data Science (WiDS), an organization that was founded at Stanford University with the goal of inspiring and supporting women in the field of data science. In the spring of my sophomore year, 2022, I organized and succesfully hosted Middlebury's first WiDS event. Recently in January 2023, I was able to organize another event. Hosting these events, I have been able to invite and hear about the work of experts such as Dr. Elena Tej Grewal, founder of Data 2 the People and former Head of Data Science at Airbnb, and Professor Allison Koenecke, Assistant Professor of Information Science at Cornell University and one of Forbes' 2022 30 under 30 in science, as well as that of many Middlebury Professors and alumni. My goal is to show Middlebury students how widely applicable data science is, and showcase what professors and recent graduates are doing in the field of data science. My hope is that students find inspiration from these speakers in either their own research and/or feel more connected to the vast world of data science.
